# PainPoint Framework

1. **Commas are not good**
   - They dilute. Start w/ the problem, just 1 until someone says YES THATS ME!

2. **Pain Point**
   - Start with a problem they relate to, and you have them hooked.

3. **Solution**
   - Position your product as solution.
   - How we help the positive result (ie: allow them to focus on what they do best).

4. **Dream Outcome**
   - THIS will happen if you work with us.

5. **Negative Stakes**
   - Paint the stakes if they don't work with us.

6. **CALL TO ACTION**
   - They need to be told what action to take.
   - Need to specifically tell people what to do and what action to take (People will go where we tell them to go‚Ä¶).
   - They are wondering at the last second is this the right decision.
   - Reassure them this is the right decision for them.
   - Have to give someone a win or lose scenario if they buy the product or not.

## Top 10 Biggest Pain Points in Production Decoration Businesses

### 1. No Single Source of Truth

Orders, artwork, pricing, approvals, vendor POs, production notes, and inventory all live in different places.

Result:

- Constant miscommunication
- ‚ÄúWhich version is correct?‚Äù
- Time wasted searching
- Costly production errors

This is the root of almost every other issue.

---

### 2. Production Scheduling Chaos

Most shops schedule using:

- Whiteboards
- Spreadsheets
- Magnets
- Verbal communication

They cannot see:

- Real capacity
- Machine availability
- Department bottlenecks
- Rush job impact

Owners are guessing.

---

### 3. Manual Repetitive Data Entry

The same order details are entered:

- In quotes
- In production sheets
- In invoices
- In inventory systems

This creates:

- Errors
- Wasted time
- Burnout
- Inconsistent records

---

### 4. Lack of Real-Time Job Visibility

The most common question in every shop:

‚ÄúWhere is this order?‚Äù

Without:

- Live status tracking
- Department visibility
- Clear task ownership

Everyone interrupts everyone.

---

### 5. Margin Leakage

Shops lose money because:

- Labor isn‚Äôt tracked
- Machine time isn‚Äôt measured
- Rush jobs disrupt efficiency
- Discounts aren‚Äôt documented
- Reprints eat profit

Most owners do not know true profit per job.

---

### 6. Pricing Complexity and Inconsistency

Decoration pricing is layered:

- Stitch count
- Screen setups
- Wash types
- Multi-location prints
- Color breaks
- Size breaks

Without structured pricing matrices:

- Sales underquotes
- Managers override pricing
- Margins erode

---

### 7. Artwork and Approval Bottlenecks

Artwork versions float in:

- Email threads
- Google Drive folders
- Desktop downloads

No structured approval tracking leads to:

- Printing wrong versions
- Delays
- Reprints
- Client disputes

---

### 8. Inventory Blind Spots

Blanks run out mid-job.

Dye lots don‚Äôt match.

Specialty materials aren‚Äôt tracked.

Without integrated inventory awareness:

- Production stalls
- Rush shipments cost more
- Clients get delayed

---

### 9. Owner Dependency and Bottlenecking

In most shops:

- The owner approves pricing
- The owner manages scheduling
- The owner resolves disputes
- The owner understands the system

If the owner leaves for a week, things slow down.

The system is not scalable.

---

### 10. Scaling Breaks the System

What works at:

- 5 employees
- 50 orders per week

Breaks at:

- 20 employees
- 300 orders per week

Because:

- Processes are informal
- Knowledge is tribal
- Systems are patched together
- Reporting is weak

Growth exposes fragility.

---

They are bad at structured operations.

Their pain is not printing.

It is managing complexity.
